About

A modeller grounded in the field

I bring an agricultural-engineering foundation together with a Master's in climate project management, working where groundwater modelling, water quality, and climate-change impact meet.

My research centres on groundwater modelling (MODFLOW), aquifer vulnerability and recharge assessment, and climate-change analysis under RCP and CMIP6 scenarios, drawing on GIS, remote sensing, and machine-learning methods. What draws me to this field is the gap between what a model says and what a decision needs — and closing it is what I want a PhD to be about.

Original research · in preparation, Q1 target

Remote-sensing green & blue crop-water accounting in the Indus Basin

A validated Random-Forest crop-mapping pipeline combined with FAO WaPOR evapotranspiration and CHIRPS rainfall, used to partition green and blue crop-water use and derive virtual water footprints and water productivity across Pakistan's Indus Basin.

MSc thesis · in progress

Grey water footprint of agricultural nitrogen leaching

Canterbury Plains aquifer under CMIP6 scenarios

Grey water-footprint accounting of nitrogen leaching from irrigated farming into a highly productive but vulnerable gravel aquifer, coupled with CMIP6 climate-scenario forcing to ask how climate change reshapes the water needed to assimilate agricultural nitrogen.

In progress · RUDN

Proposed PhD direction

Coupling groundwater flow, nitrate transport & climate

general direction — adaptable to a host group

Couple groundwater flow and solute (nitrate) transport to represent travel times and attenuation, propagate CMIP6 projections through recharge to assess future aquifer vulnerability, and test management — managed aquifer recharge and nitrogen-loading options — for real mitigation.

Open to supervision · 2027
